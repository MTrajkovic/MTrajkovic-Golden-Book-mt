    In modern capitalist economies, the currency is also a simple public monopoly. As long as money has existed, someone has tried to tamper with its value.
    A history of counterfeiting, as well as that of independence from colonial and economic rule, is another way of telling the history of “money as a creature of the state.”
    This historical understanding of the origins and nature of money illuminates the economic possibilities under different institutional monetary arrangements in the modern world.
